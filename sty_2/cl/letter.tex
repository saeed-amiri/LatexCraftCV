Dear Prof. Dr.Jörg Behler,

\vspace{1em}

I am writing to apply for the Postdoctoral Researcher position in your group. I hold a PhD in theoretical physics from the University of Göttingen. My background is in atomistic simulations, electronic structure calculations, and machine learning. I am interested in machine learning potentials because they sit at the intersection of these areas.

\vspace{1em}

During my Master's degree, I worked with density functional theory. I used SIESTA to study the electronic structure of phosphorene, a two-dimensional material. I calculated band structures and quantum transmission in the presence of impurities and defects. I used Fortran for the analysis and post-processing of the data.

\vspace{1em}

In my doctoral work, I performed molecular dynamics simulations of atomic friction. I used Python and C on HPC clusters. I studied how anharmonicity and defects affect energy dissipation. I also took part in workshops and courses in density functional theory and solid-state physics. All calculations were performed on GWDG servers.

\vspace{1em}

As a postdoctoral researcher at the University of Bremen, I continued working with molecular dynamics. I used GROMACS and LAMMPS to study interfaces between water, oil, nanoparticles, and surfactants. All computations were performed on the HLRN supercomputer. I also collaborated with an experimental group and joined them for measurements in Grenoble and at DESY.

\vspace{1em}

More recently, I completed training in data science and machine learning. I learned to work with large datasets and to build predictive models. I have used both supervised and unsupervised ML methods and applied them to time-series data from automotive sensors.

\vspace{1em}

In research, I repeatedly saw valuable data, models, and code become difficult to reproduce or maintain once a responsible person moved on. Git-based code versioning was sometimes present, but data versioning, reproducibility, and operational maintainability were frequently missing, even when expensive HPC and supercomputer workloads were involved. That experience is why I intentionally trained in data science, MLOps, cloud practices, containerization, and related tooling: to build workflows that remain usable, traceable, and sustainable over time.

\vspace{1em}

I am interested in your group because you work at the intersection of electronic structure, atomistic simulations, and machine learning. I would like to contribute to the development of scientific software and to collaborative projects within the Research Alliance Ruhr.

\vspace{1em}

Thank you for considering my application. I would be grateful for the opportunity to discuss my background further.

\vspace{1em}

Kind regards,\\

\vspace{1em}
Saeed Amiri,\\
